## Cold outreach draft

A message Maya would plausibly reply to. Written to her, not to "CHROs at 1,000-person companies."

**Subject:** The slide your CFO will actually read

**Body:**

> Hi Maya,
>
> Heard you on the Conference Board call last month. Your line about engagement decks getting submitted as appendix material to the board stuck.
>
> I'm building [Product Name] specifically for the QBR-credibility problem. Not another engagement dashboard. The pitch is: outcome-tied people data your CFO will read like he reads the finance slides.
>
> Three CHROs from the Council on People Strategy are piloting now. If your November board has a "workforce productivity TBD" slide on it, I'd love 20 minutes to show you what they're putting on theirs.
>
> If now isn't the right time, reply "later" and I'll circle back after your November board, no follow-up in between.
>
> — [Founder name]

**Things to check before sending:**

- The opener references something specific she'd recognize (a real council, a real podcast). If you don't have a real reference, find one before sending; do not fake.
- No banned hype words ("supercharge," "unlock," "revolutionize").
- The CTA gives her an easy "no" without requiring a reply explaining why.
- The "three CHROs from [council]" line must be true at the time of sending. If you don't have three CHROs piloting, change the number or remove the line.

## Notes for the founder / PMM

Things the research surfaced that don't fit elsewhere but matter:

- **Vocabulary list of words to use in copy** (lifted verbatim from sources): "the 85/87 gap," "vanity metrics," "the seat at the QBR table," "outcomes, not hours," "competitive liability," "the slide my CFO will read."
- **Words to avoid:** "tracking," "monitoring," "measurement," "surveillance," "visibility into productivity." All trigger Maya's communications team within 30 seconds. Even when factually accurate, the vocabulary kills the deal.
- **Internal champion vs blocker map for the buying committee:** Champions are usually the People Analytics lead and the CFO. Blockers are usually the General Counsel (privacy) and the head of Internal Comms (employee trust). Plan the deal to convert the GC and Comms before procurement.
- **One persona segment to deprioritize for v1:** CHROs at companies under 1,000 employees. They don't have a People Analytics function and they don't sit in formal QBRs. Different sale, different product.
